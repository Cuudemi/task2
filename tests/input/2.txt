He was indeed a deplorable spectacle. In the
dim light of a foggy November day the sick room
was a gloomy spot, but it was that gaunt, wasted
face staring at me from the bed which sent a chill
to my heart. His eyes had the brightness of fever,
there was a hectic  flush upon either cheek, and dark
crusts clung to his lips; the thin hands upon the
coverlet twitched incessantly, his voice was croaking and spasmodic. He lay listlessly as I entered
the room, but the sight of me brought a gleam of
recognition to his eyes.

“Well, Watson, we seem to have fallen upon evil
days,” said he in a feeble voice, but with something
of his old carelessness of manner.
